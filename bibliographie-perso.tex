    \item Transformation cellulaire + altération du métabolisme cellulaire.

        \subsubsection{Corps de l'article}
\begin{itemize}
    \item "In 1964, Epstein and Barr et al. first found EBV in BL patients. EBV was later shown to cause mononucleosis and nasopharyngeal carcinoma."
    \item "In 1953, Rowe et al. discovered adenoviruses."
    \item EBV: large double-stranded DNA.
    \item HBV: small double-stranded DNA.
    \item HCV: single-stranded RNA genomes (ribovirus).
    \item HIV: retrovirus.
    \item NOTE: RNA viruses are viruses that have a single-stranded or double-stranded RNA as their genetic material, while retroviruses are viruses that have a single-stranded RNA as their genetic material but use DNA intermediates in their life cycle.
    \item "which suggests that infection is only one factor in oncogenesis under a multifactorial environment and complex process". 
    \item \textbf{HBV:} "partially double-stranded, hepatophilic DNA virus that primarily infects human hepatocytes, causing liver diseases.", "HBV envelope encapsulates a nucleocapsid that contains partially double-stranded, relaxed circular DNA (rcDNA)", "binds to the cell surface via pre-S glycoprotein", "rcDNA genome is translocated into the nucleus and converted into covalently closed circular DNA (cccDNA)", cccDNA + pregnenomic RNA (pgRNA) + messenger RNA (mRNA), pgRNA + retrotranscription = HBV DNA = core proteins, polymerase, new rcDNA. "Viral envelope assembly takes place in multivesicular bodies (MVBs) to produce and secrete viral particles".
    \item \textbf{HPV:} "HPV is a non-enveloped, double-stranded, circular DNA virus", cancer (HPV16, HPV18), pas cancer (HPV6, HPV11), total: 200 types, " core genes that are involved in viral replication (E1 and E2) and packaging (L1 and L2)", "rest of the genes (E4, E5, E6, and E7) function in driving the cell cycle, immune escape, and viral release", "HPV predominantly infects basal epithelial cells, binding to the extracellular matrix (ECM) or cell surface", "Upon internalization, the capsid is disassembled in endosomes, and the multifunctional L2 protein directs viral DNA to the host cell nucleus", Les endosomes sont un ensemble d' organites de tri intracellulaires présents dans les cellules eucaryotes. Ils font partie de la voie de transport endocytaire issue du réseau trans-Golgi, "Once infection is established in the cell, the viral life cycle is regulated to maintain host-viral protein homeostasis.".
    \item 
\end{itemize}
